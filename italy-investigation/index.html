Problem Statement Conversion from New Opportunity to Paid CarAudit (unique per customer) in Italy has dropped from ~11–13% (mid-2025) to ~2–3% (Q1 2026). This needs urgent investigation.
TL;DR — Top findings
  1. Italian financing pipeline is essentially broken — only 3.24% approval rate vs CZ 40.98% (12× worse), 99.7% never reach a bank. Section 3a
  2. The biggest funnel drop is purchase-method → caraudit-form: 27.63% IT vs 58.57% CZ. The first wizard step is now matched with CZ; the cash-path commitment is where IT users bail. Section 3
  3. F&I follow-up process is failing IT customers — 32% of lost financings are "no answer from customer" (3.5× CZ). Italy has its own call center, so it's a channel/timing issue. Section 3a
  4. Conversion timing matches August 2025 transaction-flow deploy (multi-car cart, single active opportunity) and end-of-September radio-button CTA change. Opportunity volume keeps growing (+160%) while conversion drops — suggests a mix of UX friction + lower-quality traffic. Section 1
Top 3 actions: (1) Fix IT financing pipeline with F&I team, (2) Run Hotjar Survey B, (3) Conversation with marketing about IT traffic quality.

Conversion dropped from ~11% to ~2% in 10 months — with two clear inflection points
  • August 2025: -4.1 pp (from peak 13.57% in Jul to 9.43% in Aug) — first sharp drop, coincides with new transaction flow deploy
  • November 2025: -4.5 pp (from 9.61% in Oct to 5.16% in Nov) — second cliff. Likely point at which old-flow opportunities have all cycled through and new flow dominates.
Meanwhile, the number of new opportunities has been growing (299 in May 2025 → 872 in Mar 2026), suggesting increasing traffic but decreasing quality or increasing friction.

Possible trigger: new transaction flow (August 2025)

Hypothesis: decision paralysis from the new multi-car flow? In August 2025 we deployed a major change to the order wizard. Two big behavioral changes for customers:
  • Multi-car cart: customers can now add up to 3 cars to their cart before paying for CarAudit (previously: one car at a time)
  • Single active opportunity: a customer can have only one active opportunity / pay for only one CarAudit at a time (previously: multiple in parallel)

This timing aligns very well with the conversion decline: the first drop is in August 2025 (the deployment month, -4.1 pp), and a second cliff in November 2025 (-4.5 pp) likely reflects the point when old-flow opportunities have all cycled through. Possible mechanism:
  • Decision paralysis: "Should I add 3 cars or just continue with this one?" — users hesitate because they fear missing the option to compare
  • Commitment friction: the new "only one active opportunity" rule increases the perceived weight of each choice
  • Sunk-cost loops: users start adding cars but get stuck deciding whether to remove/replace

This needs investigation — it's the most plausible explanation for a drop that's correlated in time and not specific to a single funnel step. Worth testing in the upcoming Hotjar survey.
Secondary trigger: radio-button CTA change (end of September 2025) At the end of September 2025 we deployed a major change to the main CTA on the car detail page (radio-button selection of payment options). Across most markets this caused: more opportunities created, but with lower conversion.

Italy timing: the conversion cliff in IT happens at the end of October / start of November (Oct 9.61% → Nov 5.16%). The radio-button change was 4–5 weeks earlier, so the timing partially fits.

However, IT also shows continuing growth in opportunity volume (333 in Oct → 872 in Mar 2026 — +160%). This pattern (more opps, lower conversion) is consistent with the radio-button change attracting lower-intent traffic. This points to a marketing/traffic-quality lever, not just UX.

This is NOT a bank rejection problem — it's a customer follow-up problem In Italy, 68% of lost financings are customer-driven:
  • 32% no-answer-from-customer — F&I tries to contact, customer doesn't respond (3.5× more than CZ)
  • 22% customer-lost-interest — customer gave up (5.5× more than CZ)
  • Bank-driven rejections (low creditworthiness, debtor): 18% — comparable to CZ's 26%

This means the F&I follow-up process for Italy is failing. Italian customers initiate financing on the platform but don't engage with the manual F&I outreach (calls, emails, document requests).

Note: Carvago has its own Italian call center, so language is not the barrier. Possible explanations are:
  1. Channel mismatch: Italian customers may prefer async digital channels (WhatsApp, chat, email) over phone calls — we will test this in Survey B Q2
  2. Speed: F&I outreach takes too long — customer cools off, finds the car somewhere else, or simply forgets
  3. Trust: incoming call from unknown number after online application feels suspicious / spam-like
  4. Capacity / call timing: too few attempts, calls placed at wrong times of day, no async follow-up if call missed
The Italian financing pipeline is essentially non-functional Combined with the data above:
  • 99.7% of Italian financing applications never get assigned to a bank — only 2 of 720 records are linked to Agos (the only IT bank partner)
  • All 720 IT records have NULL Final_Type_Of_Financing__c — they're lost before a financing type is even chosen
  • CZ has 4–5 active bank partners with proper pipeline distribution; only 1.5% have no bank
  • Most IT financings are lost in the F&I follow-up phase, not at the bank submission phase
How this connects to the conversion problem This finding completes the picture:
  1. Italian customer chooses financing on the wizard
  2. Goes through financing-options → financing-form → financing-status (the GA funnel even shows IT does this better than CZ at the step level)
  3. But financing is rejected/lost in 87% of cases — usually before any bank ever sees it
  4. Customer would have to fall back to cash payment for CarAudit — but the prior survey shows 19% of IT users distrust online car purchases, so they drop instead

This is likely the single biggest contributor to the IT conversion drop. The August 2025 transaction change (Section 1) compounds this by making "single active opportunity" the rule — rejected customers can't keep multiple financing applications going in parallel.

What this means for our new survey

The prior survey already answered some of our questions at a general level. Our new Hotjar survey should go deeper and more specific to the wizard context:

  • We already know trust & process confusion are the top IT barriers — no need to re-validate this broadly
  • We need to learn what specifically about the wizard/cart triggers distrust or confusion
  • We need to distinguish "just browsing" (traffic quality) from "interested but blocked" (UX/trust issue)
  • CarAudit price is NOT a major barrier (only 5%) — we can safely exclude it from survey options

7. Proposed Approach: Two Tracks

Why this plan: The first wizard step (car-selection) is essentially a confirmation screen — both IT and CZ users spend just 7–9 seconds there. Surveying users at exit-intent on this page won't yield meaningful answers because they leave too fast. Instead we focus on:
  • Survey B (purchase-method) — users spend 35s here on average; this is where the IT-specific drop happens (27.63% vs 58.57% CZ)
  • Survey A (car detail page) — deferred. Catch users before they enter the wizard once we have Survey B data

Survey B design rationale

DecisionWhy
3 questions (2 required + 1 optional)Exit-intent context — users are leaving. Hotjar best practice: keep under 4 questions.
Q1 hesitation, Q2 channel, Q3 free textQ1 sets context (why are you hesitating). Q2 is the most directly actionable insight (channel preference for F&I), placed second so it gets the highest possible response rate after Q1. Q3 free text is the bonus for engaged respondents.
Q1 has 7 specific options + OtherEach option maps to a specific intervention (trust signals, copy, financing UX, live chat, etc.) so we can act on results directly.
Q1 includes "Just comparing"Baseline for traffic quality. If 50%+ pick this, the problem isn't UX — it's traffic intent / expectations set elsewhere.
Q1 excludes "Price too high"Customer already saw price on SRP and car detail (twice). At purchase-method (35s engagement), price is unlikely to be the trigger. Prior survey confirmed price isn't IT-specific (39% IT vs 42% avg).
10s timer OR exit-intent (whichever first)Auto-fire at 10s ensures we collect data even from users who never trigger exit-intent. Exit-intent catches the rest. Avg engagement is 35s so 10s is well within normal flow.
IT locale onlyFocused investigation; CZ is the healthy control with comparable wizard but ~60% conversion.
Variant B (alternative ordering) — consider for second iteration If response volume on Q2 (channel) ends up insufficient (because users abandon after Q1), consider re-running Survey B with the channel question first. Reasoning:
  • Hotjar typically sees 30–50% drop-off between Q1 and Q2. Channel-first would roughly double the channel sample size.
  • Channel preference is the most directly actionable insight (we know 32% no-answer is a confirmed problem).
  • Trade-off: loses contextual flow (user is hesitating, asked first about contact preference), and people who pick "prefer no contact" don't tell us why.
Recommended: run current order first. Switch to Variant B only if Q2 sample is too small after 4 weeks.
Survey A (car detail page) is deferred Don't run Survey A and Survey B in parallel — same user could hit both, polluting both datasets and creating survey fatigue. Survey A can launch after Survey B has 60+ responses (estimated 2–3 weeks).
Decision point after 2–3 weeks: If survey results + A/B tests show that targeted fixes lift the IT purchase-method → caraudit-form rate above 50% (CZ baseline 58.57%), we iterate. If not — and the problem is fundamental (e.g. 70%+ say "just browsing" or "don't trust online purchase") — then we consider a bigger cart/wizard rework with proper UX research.
